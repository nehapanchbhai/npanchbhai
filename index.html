About

Dr. Neha Panchbhai holds a Ph.D. in Cell and Molecular Biology from Georgia State University, where her doctoral research centred on cancer genomics and cellular protein–protein interactions — work demanding the precise, quantitative thinking that underpins rigorous mathematics education. She complemented her research career with nearly three years as a Teaching Assistant at Georgia State, developing her ability to make complex concepts accessible to students at every level.

Before moving into the classroom full-time, Dr. Panchbhai applied her analytical expertise as a Data Analyst, working with large-scale clinical datasets using SQL, Python, and SAS — deepening her appreciation for the real-world power of mathematical reasoning.

She currently teaches Mathematics at Richmond Public Schools in Virginia, bringing the precision and curiosity of a research scientist to every lesson. This site gathers her course materials, study guides, and lecture notes — written to be clear, rigorous, and self-contained.
